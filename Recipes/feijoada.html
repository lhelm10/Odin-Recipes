FEIJOADA

Description

Feijoada is a black bean and pork stew that is often served with farofa, toasted cassava flour.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dry black beans
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 ounces slab bacon
  • 1 pound pork ribs
  • 2 chorizo sausages
  • 1 large onion
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • water
  • farofa
  • rice
  • 3 bay leaves

Steps

  1. In a large bowl with water, soak beans overnight.
  2. In a large heavy-bottom soup pot, over medium heat, add the oil and bacon. Cook until crisp and transfer to a plate.
  3. Use the same saucepan to brown ribs and sausages in batches. Set each aside as cooked.
  4. On medium-high, saut√© onion and garlic until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add tomatoes and cook for another 3 minutes.
  5. Drain and rinse the soaked beans. Add them to the pot along with the ribs, bacon, sausages, salt, pepper, bay leave. Cover with water (about 8 cups).
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil and reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it cook for 2 to 2 and a half hours, or until the beans are soft.
  7. If the stew is too liquidy, uncover the saucepan and continue to cook for another 20 minutes to allow some of the liquid to evaporate.
  8. Serve with white rice and sprinkle some farofa on top.
